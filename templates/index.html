Welcome

This website provides background information on an unfamiliar and unpopular order of Arachnids: the pseudoscorpions. We will dive into fossil pseudoscorpions, paying close attention to those from the Mesozoic Era. We will present the locality data of the known and described specimens, review the work that has been done on extant and fossil pseudoscorpions, and then provide a launchpad for the first descriptions of two pseudoscorpions from the United States. This scientific discovery will provide additional context for pseudoscorpion evolution and paleogeography.


What are Pseudoscorpions?

Pseudoscorpions are small, ranging from 1 to 7 mm in length, arachnids that superficially resemble a scorpion. The pseudoscorpion, like the name suggests, is a “false scorpion” with specialized chelate pedipalps similar to the scorpions but lacking the stinging apparatus of the scorpion. Pseudoscorpions are small, ranging from one to seven millimeters in length, have zero to six eyes, that reside in decomposing leaf litter, under tree bark, and in the binding of antique texts.
